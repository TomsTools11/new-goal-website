Content strategy · June 2026

The content strategy GOAL already has, made repeatable. GOAL produces excellent client resources — a 30-day follow-up playbook and a CRM data-export hub — but they live on throwaway *.vercel.app domains, building authority GOAL doesn't market. This report is the content half of the redesign: the strategic case for an on-domain Resources Hub, the information architecture that holds it, the copy & schema playbook that makes each guide found and trusted, and the roadmap that orders what to publish. It is the source material for the new /resources/ section and the input that turns the site from a brochure into a lead-gen arm.

01

The strategic case

Why a resources section, and why now

The "stranded authority" problem

GOAL's best content currently builds equity for throwaway domains instead of the brand. The redesign is the one-time opportunity to bring it home, where it compounds.

Today

Authority leaks off-domain

checkoutgoal.com — brochure only
— no link —
goal-followup-best-practices.vercel.app
lead-export-guides.vercel.app

Guides rank (if at all) for a domain GOAL doesn't market. No internal links, no shared analytics, no path to a demo.

After redesign

Authority compounds on-brand

checkoutgoal.com/resources/
↳ links up & down
/resources/playbooks/30-day-lead-follow-up
/resources/guides/export-lead-data

Every guide strengthens the domain that ranks for "insurance leads," cross-links to money pages, carries a demo CTA.

Why content fits GOAL's buyer

  • The buyer is mid-task, not mid-funnel. Agency owners search for fixes — "my internet leads don't answer," "how do I export bind data to AgencyZoom." GOAL's product is the answer; the content demonstrates it.
  • Trust is the whole sale. A market burned by shared-lead aggregators responds to a library that openly teaches follow-up discipline and clean data hygiene — "partner," not "vendor." It reinforces the existing "old way vs. the GOAL way" narrative.
  • AI assistants are the new top of funnel. Owners ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI overviews these operational questions. Structured, schema-marked guides are how GOAL becomes the cited source.
  • Resources reduce churn. Clients who follow the cadence and export data cleanly convert more and stay. Content is a retention investment with a marketing dividend.

GOAL doesn't need to invent a content strategy. It needs to stop hiding the one it already has.

Information architecture

A shallow, scannable hub-and-spoke

URL & page structure

checkoutgoal.com/resources/ # Resources Hub — the landing/index ├─ /resources/playbooks/ # strategy & best practices │ ├─ …/30-day-lead-follow-up # ← migrate existing guide │ ├─ …/speed-to-lead │ └─ …/aged-lead-revival ├─ /resources/guides/ # CRM & data how-tos │ ├─ …/export-lead-data # ← migrate existing hub │ ├─ …/export/agencyzoom │ ├─ …/export/ricochet │ └─ …/export/lead-manager ├─ /resources/verticals/ # by line of business │ ├─ …/auto-insurance-leads │ ├─ …/home-insurance-leads │ └─ …/medicare-leads ├─ /resources/product/ # using GOAL (help/docs) │ ├─ …/quotefunnel-setup │ └─ …/reading-your-reports └─ /resources/glossary/ # definitions — long-tail + AI citations └─ …/[term]
IA principles

1. Keep it two clicks deep — hub → guide. 2. URLs describe content, not format. 3. Never change a published URL; 301-redirect if it moves. 4. Every guide links up to its category/hub and across to 2–3 siblings + one money page.

Hub page anatomy

  1. Hero — headline + one-line promise + optional search.
  2. Featured / start-here — the follow-up playbook, given pride of place.
  3. Category sections — Playbooks · Guides · By Line of Business · Using GOAL, each a card grid (reuse the proven export-hub card pattern).
  4. "Don't see what you need?" request hook (proven engagement device from the export hub).
  5. Demo CTA band — consistent conversion path at the bottom of every resource page.

The GOAL Guides Hub design reference is the intended visual spec for this hub page — map it onto the Hub / Guide / Card templates above when building.

03

Content & copy playbook

The words, written to be found and trusted

Messaging update — OKR kickoff, 19 Jun 2026

Leadership has decided to retire “exclusive” in market-facing copy in favour of “branded” — "branded visibility" and "branded leads" — because exclusive is hard to substantiate and invites EverQuote-style comparison. The paste-ready copy and query targets below predate that call; swap the term on migration (e.g. "Generate your own exclusive branded leads", "Why Branded Leads Beat Shared Leads"). Keep the underlying proof and cadence intact.

3.5 — Internal linking plan

DirectionFrom → ToAnchor-text guidance
UpGuide → category → /resources/Exact category name — "Playbooks", "CRM & Data Guides".
AcrossGuide → 2–3 sibling guidesDescriptive, varied — "speed-to-lead matters most in the first 5 minutes", not "click here".
To money pageGuide → demo / product / pricingContextual, benefit-led — "GOAL delivers exclusive leads you can actually reach". One primary, not five.
From money pagesHomepage / how-it-works → /resources/Add a "Resources" nav item + a "Learn" section. Signals depth to users and crawlers.
Rule of thumb

3–6 contextual internal links per guide, at least one to a conversion page. Keep total links per page well under 100. Diversify anchor text — repeating the same exact-match anchor everywhere reads as manipulation.

3.6 — AI / answer-engine visibility (GEO)

  • Answer in the first 1–2 sentences. Lead each section with the direct answer, then elaborate. Models lift self-contained, near-the-top statements.
  • Use real numbers and name them. "Contacted within 5 minutes → 21× more likely to qualify" is quotable; "respond quickly" is not.
  • Structure for parsing. Clear H2/H3 questions, short paragraphs, ordered steps, FAQ blocks. The playbook's 01–11 structure is already ideal.
  • Mark it up. HowTo, FAQPage, and Article schema (§3.4) make content machine-readable.
  • Build entity authority. Consistent Organization schema + a glossary defining GOAL's terms helps engines cite GOAL by name.

3.7 — Voice & copy standards

DoDon't
Lead with the agency's problem, in their words.Open with GOAL's product or company history.
Be directive and specific — "call 21+ times in week one."Hedge — "you may want to consider following up."
Back claims with numbers and name the source.Make vibes-based promises ("convert way more!").
Give something usable for free (scripts, checklists, CSV).Gate the useful part behind a demo form.
Earn the CTA at the end, once value is delivered.Interrupt the guide with sales pitches.
Plain English; define jargon (link the glossary).Acronym soup (RPC, bind rate) with no definition.
