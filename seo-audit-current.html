GOAL Performance Marketing
SEO audit · June 2026

checkoutgoal.com is a well-built brochure that search engines and AI assistants cannot find. The conversion core works — clear value prop, a demo CTA, SOC 2 / TCPA trust signals — but the site has zero search visibility: no indexable content layer, no robots.txt or sitemap, no structured data, no social tags, no canonical, no llms.txt, and no analytics. The team's own best content — a 30-day follow-up playbook and a CRM export hub — is stranded on throwaway *.vercel.app domains. Today's ~5–10 demo requests a month arrive almost entirely by word of mouth, not discovery. This audit grades the live production site as it stands and prioritises the fixes; the redesign audit and content strategy cover the build that replaces it.

04

Technical SEO

Crawl, index, deliver

Crawlability & indexability

The site is hosted as static objects on Amazon S3 behind CloudFront (server: AmazonS3). Because no robots.txt or XML sitemap exists, crawlers get no directives and no map — every page must be discovered by link-following alone, and there are only a handful of internal links to follow. Requests for clean paths (/how-it-works, /resources, /guides, /blog) return S3 AccessDenied 403s rather than content or a 404, so there is no content directory to crawl and no useful error semantics.

Note on the 403s

The 403 on /resources et al. is S3's response to a missing object key, not an intentional block — but to a crawler the effect is identical: nothing indexable lives there. The fix is to publish those paths, then add a sitemap so they're found.

URLs & routing

  • Pages resolve only at .html URLs (/how-it-works.html, /privacy-policy.html); the extensionless versions 403. This blocks the clean, descriptive URLs that content SEO depends on.
  • Asset and manifest references are relative and inconsistent (assets/favicon.ico, ../manifest.json) — brittle if pages ever move into subdirectories.
  • No canonical tags, so any future duplicate (trailing slash, .html vs. clean, query params) will split signals.

Delivery & page weight

CloudFront + HTTP/2 gives the site a fast, cacheable foundation (homepage HTML is ~33 KB). That advantage is partly spent in the <head>, which loads six external CSS/JS dependencies before content: Bootstrap 5.3 CSS & JS, Bootstrap's documentation stylesheet (getbootstrap.com/docs/5.3/assets/css/docs.css), Boxicons, animate.css, the lite-youtube module, and the Inter font requested at nine weights. Pulling a third-party project's docs CSS into production is a fragility and performance smell — it can change or vanish without notice and adds render-blocking bytes for styles the site doesn't need.

Structured data

No JSON-LD anywhere. An insurance lead-gen SaaS is a textbook fit for Organization, SoftwareApplication/Product, and FAQPage markup — none of which is present, so the site is ineligible for rich results and offers nothing structured for answer engines to lift.

On-page SEO

Titles, metas, headings, copy

Title tags & meta descriptions

PageTitle (verbatim)Meta description (verbatim)
/ GOAL | Generate Your own In-market Insurance LeadsGenerate Your own In-market Insurance Leads
/how-it-works.html How It Works | GOALGenerate Your own In-market Insurance Leads
  • Leading whitespace in both titles (" GOAL | …") — sloppy and trims oddly in SERPs.
  • Duplicate meta descriptions — every page carries the identical ~43-character string, which is also nearly the homepage H1. Descriptions should be unique per page, 150–160 chars, and benefit-led with a soft CTA.
  • Casing — "Generate Your own In-market" mixes title-case and lower-case mid-phrase; standardise.
  • No keyword targeting beyond brand — titles lead with "GOAL"; none target the operational queries agents actually search ("insurance leads for agents", "exclusive vs shared leads").

Heading structure

The homepage and how-it-works each expose two <h1> elements — a long-standing on-page error that muddies the page's primary topic. Section headings are styled in ALL-CAPS (CONVERT PROSPECTS INTO POLICIES, A BETTER WAY TO GROW); visually fine, but they read as design labels rather than intent-matched H2s. Recommendation: exactly one H1 per page stating the page's promise, with a logical H2/H3 nesting beneath it.

Copy & trust signals (the good part)

The marketing copy is clear and differentiated: "comprehensive marketing technology platform… attract, capture, and convert insurance leads with profitable ROI," the "old way vs. the GOAL way" frame, SOC 2 / CMS / TCPA compliance badges, integration logos, and a prominent demo CTA with phone number. This is a strong conversion skeleton — it simply has no audience arriving from search to convert.

Messaging note — OKR kickoff, 19 Jun 2026

Leadership is retiring "exclusive" in favour of "branded" ("branded visibility / branded leads"). Fold this into the rewrite of titles, metas, and H1s rather than re-editing the old copy twice.

Content & authority

The stranded-authority problem

GOAL's single biggest SEO liability isn't a missing tag — it's that the brand's best content earns authority for domains it will never market.

Today

Authority leaks off-domain

checkoutgoal.com — brochure only
— no link —
goal-followup-best-practices.vercel.app
lead-export-guides.vercel.app

Two genuinely good guides rank (if at all) for throwaway domains. No internal links from the main site, no shared analytics, no path to a demo.

The fix

Authority compounds on-brand

checkoutgoal.com/resources/
↳ links up & down
/resources/playbooks/30-day-lead-follow-up
/resources/guides/export-lead-data

Migrate the guides on-domain and 301 the old URLs. Every guide then strengthens the domain that should rank for "insurance leads" and carries a demo CTA.

The full migration plan, IA, and copy templates live in the Content Strategy report. This audit's job is only to flag the leak as the highest-value content fix available.

07

AI / answer-engine visibility

GEO & AEO — currently zero

Agency owners increasingly ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews their operational questions ("how do I follow up on internet insurance leads", "best CRM for insurance agents"). GOAL is not a candidate to be cited in any of those answers today, for three compounding reasons:

  • No machine-readable content. llms.txt is absent (403) and there is no schema, so there is nothing structured for a model to ground on.
  • No quotable substance. Answer engines lift specific, self-contained statements with named numbers. The brochure offers marketing claims, not the "21+ attempts in week one" kind of fact that gets quoted.
  • No entity authority. Without consistent Organization markup or a glossary defining GOAL's terms, engines have no reason to associate the brand with the topic.

This is greenfield: every guide published with answer-first copy and FAQPage/HowTo schema is a fresh chance to become the cited source. The method is detailed in Content Strategy §3.6.

08

Analytics & measurement

You can't optimise what you can't see

No analytics tag, tag manager, or pixel was found on any page, and there is no Search Console footprint. In practice the team's only measurement of the website today is counting demo-request messages as they land in a Slack channel — there is no visibility into traffic volume, sources, queries, on-site behaviour, or conversion rate. That makes the current ~5–10 demos/month an output with no denominator: it's impossible to say whether it reflects 200 visitors or 2,000.

Baseline before launch

Even before the redesign ships, stand up GA4, Google Search Console, and Bing Webmaster Tools on the current site. A few weeks of real baseline (sessions, sources, demo-form conversion rate) turns the OKR target — lifting demos from 5–10 to 30–50+ — from a guess into a measured delta.

09

What's working

Don't rebuild these

  • A clear, differentiated value proposition — the "marketing technology platform, not a lead aggregator" positioning lands, and pre-empts the EverQuote comparison.
  • A working conversion path — prominent demo CTA, phone number, and a "request a demo" flow that already produces qualified word-of-mouth requests.
  • Real trust signals — SOC 2, CMS, and TCPA compliance badges plus integration logos; credible for a regulated buyer.
  • Fast static delivery — CloudFront/HTTP 2 means performance is a tuning exercise, not a re-platform.
  • Clean baseline hygiene — valid lang, responsive viewport, favicon, PWA manifest, theme-color.

The strategic read: the site converts the demand it's handed; it generates none of its own. Reports 02–03 add the demand engine on top of this working core.
