SEO audit · June 2026

The redesign is designed to fix nearly every SEO gap in the current site. A premium, on-domain content layer (a /resources/ hub), structured data and an llms.txt baked in from the start, clean descriptive URLs, and an on-page template system that makes correct SEO the default. If it ships as specified, GOAL moves from invisible to genuinely competitive in search and answer engines. The grade is conditional on a short list of SEO launch gates — removing the staging noindex, submitting the sitemap, 301-redirecting the old guide URLs, and getting the programmatic pages right. This report audits the SEO blueprint and sets the pre-launch bar.

02

Scope & method

A blueprint audit, by necessity

Important — staging is not publicly accessible

The redesigned site lives on a password-protected staging domain behind a private path. It cannot be crawled from outside, so this is a design-stage audit: it assesses the SEO of the architecture as specified, plus a pre-launch readiness bar. It should be re-run as a live SEO crawl the moment staging access is shared (an action already flagged in the OKR kickoff).

  • Inputs: the Client Resources Strategy (IA, copy, schema, internal-linking, and technical-SEO specs) and the SEO direction set in the OKR kickoff — an on-domain resources hub, structured data + llms.txt pre-built, and programmatic carrier×state landing pages.
  • What this report evaluates: whether that design is SEO-sound, what it will and won't achieve in search and answer engines, and exactly which SEO items must be verified or fixed before launch.
  • What it can't yet do: crawl the live pages, confirm the indexing directives, or check rankings — those need the staging URL, then the live site.

04

Architecture & URLs

The IA is right; protect it

The proposed information architecture is a shallow, two-clicks-deep hub-and-spoke — exactly what content SEO wants. Descriptive, format-agnostic URLs; a hub that links down to categories and up from every guide; a 301 discipline for anything that moves.

checkoutgoal.com/resources/ # hub — landing/index ├─ /resources/playbooks/ # strategy & best practices ├─ /resources/guides/ # CRM & data how-tos ├─ /resources/verticals/ # by line of business (programmatic) ├─ /resources/product/ # using GOAL (help/docs) └─ /resources/glossary/ # long-tail + AI citations
Strengths

Clean, descriptive URLs are part of the spec. The hub-and-spoke keeps crawl depth low, the breadcrumb plan gives every page an "up" link, and the "never change a published URL" rule prevents the slow equity bleed that kills most content libraries.

Watch-items

  • Canonical on filtered/paginated views. Once the hub has category filters or pagination, each variant needs a self-referencing canonical (or a consolidated index) to avoid splitting signals — the one IA risk that scales with the library.
  • Breadcrumb + BreadcrumbList parity. Visual breadcrumb and JSON-LD breadcrumb must match the real URL path on every template.
  • Trailing-slash consistency. Pick one convention and 301 the other; don't let /resources and /resources/ both resolve.
05

On-page & structured data

Templates that are right by default

The redesign templatises the on-page basics the current site lacks: title ≤ 60 chars, meta 150–160, exactly one H1, searcher-term-first then "| GOAL". Because these live in reusable Hub / Guide / Card templates, every new page inherits correct SEO rather than relying on an author to remember it. That's the single most durable on-page win in the whole plan.

Schema coverage is well-matched to page type — and broader than the current site's zero:

CRM / data guidesHowTo

Recommended additions

Extend the schema set as features land: Offer/AggregateOffer for the pricing tiers, Review / AggregateRating once video testimonials are permissioned (the social-proof work from the kickoff), and VideoObject for the client-led webinar and testimonial clips. Each unlocks a rich result the brochure can't earn. Validate every template in Google's Rich Results Test before it ships.

Full paste-ready copy, title/meta/H1 patterns, and JSON-LD snippets are in the Content Strategy playbook. Remember the "exclusive" → "branded" copy swap before publish.

06

AI / answer-engine readiness

Built for citation, not just ranking

This is where the redesign genuinely leads. An llms.txt is pre-built, and the content method (answer-first sentences, named numbers, FAQ blocks, HowTo steps) is exactly what ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews lift. Against a current site with zero AI visibility, this is a step-change.

  • Keep llms.txt curated, not auto-dumped. It should point engines at the canonical guides and the glossary — a short, intentional map, refreshed as the library grows.
  • Entity authority. Consistent Organization markup with sameAs (LinkedIn, Crunchbase, socials) plus a glossary defining GOAL's terms gives engines a reason to cite the brand by name.
  • FAQPage everywhere it's honest. Build FAQ blocks from the guide's real questions; these are disproportionately quoted in AI answers.
  • Lead with the answer. Each guide's first 1–2 sentences should state the direct answer, then elaborate — self-contained, near-the-top statements are what get lifted into AI overviews.
07

Programmatic SEO at scale

Carrier × state landing pages — the big lever and the big risk

The plan to spin up a landing page per carrier × state is the redesign's largest organic-upside bet — and its largest risk. Done well, it captures enormous long-tail intent ("auto insurance leads in Texas", "[carrier] agent leads"). Done carelessly, it generates thousands of near-duplicate, thin pages that dilute the domain and invite a quality demotion.

Upside

Long-tail capture

One template, hundreds of high-intent queries. Each page can link up to the relevant vertical guide and across to the demo — turning search into pipeline at scale.

Risk

Thin / duplicate at scale

Templated pages that differ only by a place-name are the classic doorway-page trap. Index bloat slows crawling of the pages that matter.

Guardrails — apply before publishing the set

1. Each page needs genuinely unique content (local stats, carrier specifics), not just a swapped noun. 2. Start small and measure; noindex variants that can't be made substantive. 3. Segment programmatic URLs into their own sitemap so you can watch indexation rate. 4. Canonical correctly; never let two near-identical pages compete. 5. Roll out in batches — don't drop 2,000 pages on day one.

08

Indexing & launch

Don't let a redesign de-index the site

Staging is correctly protected by a password and should carry noindex. The number-one launch failure mode for redesigns is shipping that noindex — or a Disallow: / — straight into production and quietly de-indexing the whole site. Make removal an explicit, verified launch step, alongside the migration redirects.

  • Remove noindex and the staging password; confirm production robots.txt does not contain Disallow: /.
  • 301-redirect the two *.vercel.app guide URLs (and any old .html URLs) to their new homes; keep slugs stable.
  • Set canonicals to the production domain; submit the sitemap in Search Console & Bing; request indexing on key pages.
  • Spot-check a sample of live pages for status 200 and the correct canonical before announcing.
Why it matters

Every SEO advantage in this report — schema, llms.txt, clean URLs, the content library — counts for nothing if the launch carries a stray noindex into production. This one checklist is the difference between a relaunch that compounds and one that resets to zero.

09

Measurement & conversion pages

Measure search; make conversion pages rank

Search measurement (from day one)

The current site is flying blind; the redesign must not. Google Search Console (and Bing Webmaster Tools), plus a basic analytics baseline for the demo-conversion rate, are non-negotiable at launch — so the demo-request KPI has a real denominator and the OKR can be measured rather than guessed at. Carry over the baseline established on the current site (see Current-Site Audit §8).

Conversion pages as SEO surfaces

  • The demo page must be indexable content, not just a form — a real H1, value copy, and ideally Service/Offer schema, so it can rank for "book a demo / GOAL pricing" intent on its own merits.
  • Marketplace messaging (launching July). The new in-product marketplace needs its own planned section/URL and schema, with copy aligned to the "branded leads + curated marketplace" narrative — so the site doesn't market only the clicks product.
  • Keep money pages discoverable. Pricing, product, and how-it-works pages should each have unique titles/metas and internal links from the resources library — they're conversion and ranking surfaces.
