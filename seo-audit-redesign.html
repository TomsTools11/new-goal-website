SEO audit · June 2026

The redesign is engineered to fix nearly every gap in the current site — on paper. A premium, on-domain content layer (a /resources/ hub), a Strapi CMS feeding programmatic landing pages, schema and an llms.txt baked in from the start, clean URLs on Vercel, and a segmented demo flow. If it ships as specified, GOAL moves from invisible to genuinely competitive in search and answer engines. But the grade is conditional: three things decide whether the design's SEO potential survives contact with production — how the JS site renders, whether staging's noindex/password is cleanly removed at launch, and whether analytics ships on day one. This report audits the blueprint and sets the pre-launch bar.

02

Scope & method

A blueprint audit, by necessity

Important — staging is not publicly accessible

The redesigned site lives on a password-protected staging domain behind a private path. It cannot be crawled from outside, so this is a design-stage audit: it assesses the SEO of the architecture as specified, plus a pre-launch readiness bar. It should be re-run as a live technical crawl the moment staging access is shared (an action already flagged in the OKR kickoff).

  • Inputs: the Client Resources Strategy (IA, copy, schema, internal-linking, and technical-SEO specs) and the technical direction surfaced in the OKR kickoff — Strapi CMS on a VPS, Supabase, a Vercel-hosted front end, dark/premium theme, interactive product components, schema + llms.txt pre-built, and programmatic carrier×state landing pages.
  • What this report evaluates: whether that design is SEO-sound, what it will and won't achieve, and exactly which items must be verified or fixed before launch.
  • What it can't yet do: measure real rendered HTML, Core Web Vitals, or live response headers — those require the staging URL.

04

Architecture & URLs

The IA is right; protect it

The proposed information architecture is a shallow, two-clicks-deep hub-and-spoke — exactly what content SEO wants. Descriptive, format-agnostic URLs; a hub that links down to categories and up from every guide; a 301 discipline for anything that moves.

checkoutgoal.com/resources/ # hub — landing/index ├─ /resources/playbooks/ # strategy & best practices ├─ /resources/guides/ # CRM & data how-tos ├─ /resources/verticals/ # by line of business (programmatic) ├─ /resources/product/ # using GOAL (help/docs) └─ /resources/glossary/ # long-tail + AI citations
Strengths

Clean URLs are already configured on Vercel (cleanUrls). The hub-and-spoke keeps crawl depth low, the breadcrumb plan gives every page an "up" link, and the "never change a published URL" rule prevents the slow equity bleed that kills most content libraries.

Watch-items

  • Canonical on filtered/paginated views. Once the hub has category filters or pagination, each variant needs a self-referencing canonical (or a consolidated index) to avoid splitting signals — the one IA risk that scales with the library.
  • Breadcrumb + BreadcrumbList parity. Visual breadcrumb and JSON-LD breadcrumb must match the real URL path on every template.
  • Trailing-slash consistency. Pick one convention and 301 the other; don't let /resources and /resources/ both resolve.
05

On-page & structured data

Templates that are right by default

The redesign templatises the on-page basics the current site lacks: title ≤ 60 chars, meta 150–160, exactly one H1, searcher-term-first then "| GOAL". Because these live in reusable Hub / Guide / Card templates, every new page inherits correct SEO rather than relying on an author to remember it. That's the single most durable on-page win in the whole plan.

Schema coverage is well-matched to page type — and broader than the current site's zero:

Recommended additions

Extend the schema set as features land: Offer/AggregateOffer for the pricing tiers, Review / AggregateRating once video testimonials are permissioned (the social-proof work from the kickoff), and VideoObject for the client-led webinar and testimonial clips. Each unlocks a rich result the brochure can't earn. Validate every template in Google's Rich Results Test before it ships.

Full paste-ready copy, title/meta/H1 patterns, and JSON-LD snippets are in the Content Strategy playbook. Remember the "exclusive" → "branded" copy swap before publish.

06

AI / answer-engine readiness

Built for citation, not just ranking

This is where the redesign genuinely leads. An llms.txt is pre-built, and the content method (answer-first sentences, named numbers, FAQ blocks, HowTo steps) is exactly what ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews lift. Against a current site with zero AI visibility, this is a step-change.

  • Keep llms.txt curated, not auto-dumped. It should point engines at the canonical guides and the glossary — a short, intentional map, refreshed as the library grows.
  • Entity authority. Consistent Organization markup with sameAs (LinkedIn, Crunchbase, socials) plus a glossary defining GOAL's terms gives engines a reason to cite the brand by name.
  • FAQPage everywhere it's honest. Build FAQ blocks from the guide's real questions; these are disproportionately quoted in AI answers.
  • Don't let JS hide it. GEO depends entirely on §08 — if the answer-first content isn't in the server-rendered HTML, no model will ever see it.
07

Programmatic SEO at scale

Strapi carrier × state pages — the big lever and the big risk

The plan to spin up a landing page per carrier × state from Strapi is the redesign's largest organic-upside bet — and its largest risk. Done well, it captures enormous long-tail intent ("auto insurance leads in Texas", "[carrier] agent leads"). Done carelessly, it generates thousands of near-duplicate, thin pages that dilute the domain and invite a quality demotion.

Upside

Long-tail capture

One template, hundreds of high-intent queries. Each page can link up to the relevant vertical guide and across to the demo — turning search into pipeline at scale.

Risk

Thin / duplicate at scale

Templated pages that differ only by a place-name are the classic doorway-page trap. Index bloat slows crawling of the pages that matter.

Guardrails — apply before publishing the set

1. Each page needs genuinely unique data (local stats, carrier specifics), not just a swapped noun. 2. Start small and measure; noindex variants that can't be made substantive. 3. Segment programmatic URLs into their own sitemap so you can watch indexation rate. 4. Canonical correctly; never let two near-identical pages compete. 5. Roll out in batches — don't drop 2,000 pages on day one.

08

Rendering & launch hygiene

The two things most likely to sink the launch

Rendering — the critical dependency

The new site is a dark-themed, JavaScript-driven experience with interactive product components. SEO and GEO both depend on one question: is the content in the HTML the server returns, or is it painted by client-side JS after load? If it's client-rendered, crawlers and AI models may see an empty shell — and every schema, llms.txt, and copy advantage above is wasted.

Requirement

Render resource and content pages with SSR or static generation (on Vercel, Next.js SSG/ISR is the natural fit). Strapi content must be fetched at build/request time and embedded in the initial HTML — not hydrated client-side. Verify with "View Source" (content present?) and Search Console's URL Inspection (rendered HTML matches?). Keep client JS on content pages minimal for LCP/INP.

Launch hygiene — the avoidable disaster

Staging is correctly protected by a password and should carry noindex. The number-one launch failure mode for redesigns is shipping that noindex — or a Disallow: / — straight into production and quietly de-indexing the whole site. Make removal an explicit, verified launch step.

  • Remove noindex and the staging password; confirm production robots.txt does not contain Disallow: /.
  • 301-redirect the two *.vercel.app guide URLs (and any old .html URLs) to their new homes; keep slugs stable.
  • Set canonicals to the production domain; submit the sitemap in Search Console & Bing; request indexing on key pages.
  • Spot-check a sample of live pages for rendered content, status 200, and correct canonical before announcing.
09

Analytics & the conversion path

Measure the North Star; route the demand

Measurement (ship on day one)

The current site is flying blind; the redesign must not. GA4 and Search Console are non-negotiable at launch, with event tracking on the demo flow (start, step completion, submission) so the demo-request KPI has a real denominator. An intent tool such as RB2B can layer on de-anonymised account visibility for sales. Without this on day one, the OKR can't be measured — only guessed at.

The demo flow & marketplace

The segmented demo flow (qualifying fields → Cal.com booking → Close + Slack) is a strong, low-friction conversion design — let prospects book instantly rather than waiting on manual Slack routing. SEO watch-items:

  • The demo page must have crawlable content, not just a form — a real H1, value copy, and ideally Service/Offer schema, so it can rank for "book a demo / GOAL pricing" intent.
  • Marketplace messaging (launching July). The new in-product marketplace needs its own planned section/URL and schema, with copy aligned to the "branded leads + curated marketplace" narrative — so the site doesn't market only the clicks product.
  • Form isn't the only content. Keep the page indexable independent of the JS form widget (ties back to §08).
