Overview · June 2026

Turning the GOAL website from a brochure into a lead-gen arm. Today checkoutgoal.com has zero search visibility and converts only the word-of-mouth demand it's handed — ~5–10 demo requests a month, unmeasured. This workstream — the quarter's priority OKR — pairs a premium redesign with the SEO and content foundation it has never had. This overview links the three deliverables: a full audit of the current site, a pre-launch audit of the redesign, and the content strategy for the on-domain Resources Hub that turns search into pipeline.

03

OKR & North Star

What we're measured against

From the OKR kickoff (19 Jun 2026), this is framed as the quarter's most important objective — the foundation for growth into 2027.

  • Objective — launch a redesigned checkoutgoal.com that represents GOAL as the premium, ~$15M-run-rate company it is, and converts visitors into demo requests.
  • Committed KR — ship V1 (website + segmented demo flow + SEO/GEO foundation) by the target date; an ideal V1 lands in ~30 days to coincide with the in-product marketplace launch.
  • North-Star metric — increase monthly demo requests from a 5–10 baseline to 30–50+ (a 3–5× lift).
  • Grounding benchmark — at the SaaS-median 1.5% form-conversion rate, ~10,000 monthly visitors ≈ 150 demos — so the target is ambitious but realistic.

Prerequisite: establish a real traffic/conversion baseline on the current site (GA4 + Search Console) so the lift can be measured, not guessed. See Current-Site Audit §8.
